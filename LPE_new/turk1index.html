What is the purpose of this study?

You are invited to participate in an Internet-based research study on self-descriptions. This study will enroll approximately 600 adults.

What can I expect as a participant in this study?

As part of this Internet-based study, you will be asked to rate how well various statements describe you. The entire study will take approximately 10 minutes to complete.

What are the possible risks of participating in this study?

There are minimal risks associated with participation in this study. Risks include a breach of confidentiality, and that you might get tired or frustrated. To reduce these risks we will not log your Internet Protocol (IP) address, and all data will be stored in a password-protected file. Also, you can choose to discontinue your participation at anytime.

What are the possible benefits of participating in this study?

You will not benefit directly from participating in this study. However, your participation might benefit other people in the future, and you may feel satisfied from contributing to research.

Will there be financial compensation for participating in this study?

After you complete this study, $1 will be credited to your Amazon Mechanical Turk account.

How will my privacy be protected?

No identifying information will be collected from you, and your Internet Protocol (IP) address will not be recorded. All information you provide will be stored in a password-protected file.

Who can I contact if I have questions about this study or about my rights as a research participant?

For more information about this study, contact the Principal Investigator, Morton Ann Gernsbacher, PhD at +1 608-262-6989 or via email at MAGernsb@wisc.edu.

For more information about your rights as a participant in a research study, contact the University of Wisconsin-Madison Education Research and Social & Behavioral Science IRB Office at +1 608-263-2320 or email lmlarson@ls.admin.wisc.edu.
