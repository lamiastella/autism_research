Title of the Study: Concepts and Images Study

Principal Investigator:
Morton Ann Gernsbacher, PhD +1 608-262-6989 MAGernsb@wisc.edu

What is the purpose of this study?

You are invited to participate in an Internet-based research study on concepts and images. This study will enroll approximately 400 adults.

What can I expect as a participant in this study?

As part of this Internet-based study, you will be asked to classify concepts into two categories and classify visual images into two categories. You will also be asked to complete a short questionnaire. The entire study will take approximately 10 minutes to complete.

What are the possible risks of participating in this study?

There are minimal risks associated with participation in this study. There is a small risk of loss of privacy if your Internet connection is not secure. There is a small risk that you might get frustrated if you have computer or Internet problems. Also, you can choose to withdraw from the study at anytime.

What are the possible benefits of participating in this study?

You will not benefit directly from participating in this study. However, your participation might benefit other people in the future, and you may feel satisfied from contributing to research.

Will there be financial compensation for participating in this study?

After you complete this study, $1 will be credited to your Amazon Mechanical Turk account.

How will my privacy be protected?

No identifying information will be collected from you, and all data will be stored in a password-protected file.

Who can I contact if I have questions about this study or about my rights as a research participant?

For more information about this study, contact the Principal Investigator, Morton Ann Gernsbacher, PhD at +1 608-262-6989 or via email at MAGernsb@wisc.edu.

For more information about your rights as a participant in a research study, contact the University of Wisconsin Madison Education Research and Social and Behavioral Science IRB Office at +1 608-263-2320 or email lmlarson@ls.wisc.edu.
